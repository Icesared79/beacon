Signal Intelligence
What Beacon Measures
Beacon detects five distinct distress signals across residential and commercial properties. Each signal is independently verified and scored to identify homeowners facing financial or legal pressure relevant to acquisition, counseling, or community capital deployment.
SIGNAL 01
Pre-Foreclosure Filing
Detected when a lender files a lis pendens or notice of default against a property. This is the earliest court-recorded indicator that a mortgage is in serious delinquency and legal action has begun.
Why it matters: Homeowners in pre-foreclosure face a defined legal timeline. Early identification creates a window for intervention before auction scheduling.
SIGNAL 02
Bankruptcy Petition
Detected when a property owner files for Chapter 7, 11, or 13 bankruptcy protection. Beacon cross-references court filing data with property ownership records to confirm the debtor holds real property.
Why it matters: Bankruptcy often signals cascading financial distress. Property held by a debtor in bankruptcy may be subject to liquidation or restructuring.
SIGNAL 03
Tax Delinquency
Detected when property tax records show delinquent or unpaid balances beyond standard grace periods. Beacon tracks both municipal tax liens and delinquency status from county assessor data.
Why it matters: Unpaid property taxes create compounding liens with priority over mortgages. Tax delinquency is one of the strongest predictors of motivated disposition.
SIGNAL 04
Probate or Estate Filing
Detected when a property owner is identified in probate court records, indicating the property is part of an estate proceeding. Beacon matches decedent records against ownership data to flag inherited properties.
Why it matters: Inherited properties are frequently unmaintained, carry deferred taxes, and are held by heirs with no intent to occupy — creating disposition likelihood.
SIGNAL 05
UCC Lien or Code Violation
Detected when a property has a UCC financing statement filed against the owner, or active code enforcement violations on record. These indicate either encumbered assets or deferred property maintenance at a structural level.
Why it matters: UCC liens signal business-level distress attached to real property. Code violations indicate neglect and potential condemnation risk — both suggest ownership under pressure.
+
Compound signals carry more weight. When multiple signals are detected on the same property — for example, a pre-foreclosure filing combined with tax delinquency — Beacon increases the distress score proportionally. Properties with three or more active signals are flagged as high-priority.

Data Architecture
How the Data Works
Beacon is built on Atlas, Red Planet's property intelligence engine — over 93 million verified records across 556 sourced data feeds spanning all 50 states.
01
Ingest
Sourced data from property records, court filing data, state registry data, and tax records is ingested through automated pipelines on a defined schedule per source.
02
Normalize
Records are deduplicated, address-matched, and standardized against the Atlas property graph to link signals to specific parcels and owners.
03
Score
Each property receives a compound distress score based on the number, recency, and severity of active signals. More signals and more recent filings produce higher scores.

Compound Distress Scoring
Each property's score is computed by weighting active signals by recency, type severity, and overlap. A single old tax delinquency scores lower than a recent pre-foreclosure filing — but a property with both scores significantly higher than either alone. Scores range from 0 (no signals) to 100 (maximum compound distress).

Data Validation
Source Verification
Every data feed is checked against expected record counts, schema consistency, and freshness thresholds on each ingestion cycle. Feeds that fail validation are quarantined until manually reviewed.
Address Matching
Signals are linked to properties through multi-pass address normalization — standardizing street formats, unit numbers, and parcel identifiers to prevent duplicate or orphaned records.
Freshness Scoring
Each source carries a freshness timestamp. Atlas tracks time-since-last-update at the feed level to ensure downstream products reflect current conditions, not stale snapshots.
How Beacon Handles Aging Data
When a data source has not been refreshed within its expected cycle, Beacon applies a decay weight to signals derived from that source. Signals older than their source's refresh window are downgraded in scoring — they still appear, but carry less weight in the compound score. If a source exceeds twice its expected refresh interval, its signals are flagged as unconfirmed and excluded from score calculations until the next successful ingestion. This ensures Beacon never presents stale data as current intelligence.
