Reading the Heat Map

1
Color intensity = signal concentration. Deeper red means more overlapping distress signals detected in that state.
2
Office filter — scopes the map to a counselor's assigned geography. Multi-select available for regional managers.
3
Signal type filter — isolate specific distress types: foreclosure filings, tax liens, code violations, utility shutoffs, etc.
4
Click a state to drill down into county-level view. County tiles show the same heat scale at higher resolution.
5
Time range — adjust the lookback window. 30/60/90 day views show whether distress is accelerating or cooling.
6
NJ shows "Critical" — this counselor's home state has the highest concentration. 5+ overlapping signal types active.

What the Colors Mean

Each intensity level corresponds to the number of distinct distress signal types detected within the lookback window:

Low (1 signal)

Single indicator present. Monitor but not urgent.

Elevated (3 signals)

Multiple distress types. Proactive outreach recommended.

Critical (5+ signals)

Severe overlap. Immediate counselor intervention needed.

Key Elements

1
Compound Score — 0–100 severity rating. Combines signal count, signal severity, recency, and property value. Red ≥ 75, amber 50–74, teal < 50.
2
Signal pills — color-coded by severity. Red = legal/imminent action. Amber = warning indicators. Teal = monitoring only.
3
Status workflow — New → Contacted → In Review → Escalated → Resolved. Counselors update as they engage with the homeowner.
4
Filters — narrow by score threshold, status, county, signal type. Saved filter presets per counselor.
5
Search — full-text across address, owner name, and parcel ID. Results update as you type.
6
Export — download filtered results as CSV for reporting or offline outreach planning. Respects active filters.

How Counselors Use This View

1
Timeline — chronological signal history reads top-to-bottom. Red dots = severe, amber = warning, gray = context events.
2
Trend chart — the rising line shows distress accelerating. A flat or dropping line means the situation is stabilizing.
3
Score ring — the 92 tells the counselor: this case is urgent. The ring color matches severity (red ≥ 75).
4
Conversation prep — the counselor now knows: foreclosure was filed 6 days ago, there's a tax lien, and a code violation. They can open with specific, informed language.
5
Equity at risk — $72.6K estimated equity. This frames the urgency: the homeowner has real wealth to preserve.
6
Signal cards — quick-reference summaries with detection dates. Active signals have a red left border; resolved signals are gray.

Why This Matters

Without Beacon, a counselor might reach this homeowner after the sheriff's sale date is set. With Beacon, they reach her within days of the lis pendens filing — when intervention options (loan modification, repayment plans, mediation) are still available. The signal timeline turns raw public data into actionable outreach intelligence.
